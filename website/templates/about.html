About the project

Smart Grid Data

Smart Grid Data problem

As our P&O3-engineering project at the KU Leuven, we choose to work around Smart Grid Data. In this project we're being given a demand response problem. The problem requires us to create an algorithm that efficiently charges cars, that are parked at EnergyVille, a company that specialises in research for sustainable energy. The company has a lot of solarpannels which supplies a lot of green energy that we want to put as much as possible in charging the cars. In order for us to create a efficient algorithm we have to first predict the incoming solar energy data. This is made possible with something called "machine learning"

What's machine learning?

Machine learning is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on developing algorithms and models capable of learning from data. These are clever systems that can recognize certain patterns that humans most of the times can't see. Machine learning has various applications, from image recognition and language processing to predictive analytics which we are going to use it for. For reference we compare a dumb charging strategy compared with our algorithm. The "dumb" strategy charges the cars immediately when the it's plugged in and it stops when the battery is full. This method okay for use at home but not when charging 20 cars on a day. That's why you can see on the right that the charging times are more spread during the day and more at noon, because then the sun is at it's highest.
